Overview

The Solar System consists of the Sun and everything gravitationally bound to it: eight planets, five dwarf planets, hundreds of moons, and billions of small bodies (asteroids, comets, Kuiper Belt objects). Formed ~4.6 billion years ago from a gravitational collapse of a giant molecular cloud fragment.

Physical parameters

Sol (G2V main sequence)
Age
~4.568 billion years

Dwarf planets

Known moons
200+
Asteroid Belt

Kuiper Belt

Oort Cloud

Heliopause
~120 AU, outer boundary of heliosphere
Nearest star
Proxima Centauri, 4.24 light-years

Active missions

Voyager 1 / 2 (NASA)
Active since 1977
Flagship grand tour missions. Both are now in interstellar space.
Pioneer 10 / 11 (NASA)
1972/1973 (contact lost)
First spacecraft to explore outer Solar System.
New Horizons (NASA)
Active since 2006
Pluto flyby 2015, Arrokoth flyby 2019, now exploring Kuiper Belt.
Parker Solar Probe (NASA)
Active since 2018
Studying the Sun from inside the corona.
JWST (NASA/ESA/CSA)
Active since 2021
Observes infrared from Sun-Earth L2.

Notable features

Terrestrial planets
Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars - rocky inner planets.
Gas giants
Jupiter, Saturn - hydrogen/helium dominated.
Ice giants
Uranus, Neptune - water, methane, ammonia ices.
Asteroid Belt
Rocky/metallic small bodies between Mars and Jupiter. Contains the dwarf planet Ceres.
Kuiper Belt
Ring of icy bodies beyond Neptune. Contains Pluto, Eris, and other dwarf planets.
Heliosphere
Region of space dominated by solar wind. Extends ~120 AU before interstellar medium.
Trojans
Asteroids sharing planetary orbits at L4/L5 Lagrange points. Most are Jupiter Trojans.
