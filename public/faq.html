Frequently Asked Questions

SolarSystemResource · a Corgan Studio spatial intelligence platform
Last updated April 13, 2026
What is SolarSystemResource?
SolarSystemResource is a heliocentric spatial intelligence platform showing the Sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, active spacecraft, comets, asteroids, and deep space missions in a unified 3D view at solar system scale. It is the fourth platform in the Resource Systems suite, developed by Corgan Studio alongside TerraResource (Earth), MoonResource (Moon), and MarsResource (Mars).
What is Resource Systems?
Resource Systems is the first multi-planetary spatial intelligence platform, bringing together Earth, Moon, Mars, and the solar system at large in a unified suite with live data, real-time mission tracking, and cross-body analytics. Developed by Corgan Studio, the suite includes TerraResource (terraresource.net), MoonResource (moonresource.com), MarsResource (marsresource.net), and SolarSystemResource (solarsystemresource.com). Resource Systems launched in 2025.
How is SolarSystemResource different from other solar system viewers?
SolarSystemResource is part of an integrated Resource Systems suite with shared authentication, a shared tier system, and cross-platform mission tracking. Unlike standalone solar system visualizations, users can follow a single spacecraft across Earth, Moon, Mars, and heliocentric perspectives using real NASA ephemeris data. The platform is purpose-built for solar-system-scale visualization, handling 1 to 40 AU scales natively where traditional Earth-centric 3D engines struggle.
What rendering engine does it use?
SolarSystemResource uses spacekit.js, an open-source Three.js-based rendering engine purpose-built for solar-system-scale visualization. spacekit.js was chosen over CesiumJS (used by the other three Resource Systems platforms) because Cesium is optimized for Earth-surface and planet-surface views and does not handle heliocentric 1 to 40 AU scales well. Planet positions are computed from JPL Keplerian orbital elements at the J2000 reference frame.
Are the planet positions scientifically accurate?
Yes for orbital paths and relative distances. Planets are rendered from JPL orbital elements (semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination, epoch) that match the J2000 reference frame. Planet body sizes, however, are not rendered to scale relative to their orbits. If they were, Earth would be smaller than a single pixel at a zoom where its orbit is visible. Like every solar system visualization, SolarSystemResource uses constant-pixel-size billboards so bodies remain visible regardless of camera distance. This is a deliberate visualization choice, not an error.
What is the Deep Space Network feed?
SolarSystemResource displays live data from NASA's Deep Space Network (DSN), showing which antennas at Goldstone (California), Madrid (Spain), and Canberra (Australia) are actively communicating with deep space spacecraft. The feed updates continuously with dish status, target identification, signal strength, and round-trip light time. The same feed is shared across all four Resource Systems platforms via a shared Supabase edge function.
What layers are available?
Current layers include the Sun and eight planets with accurate Keplerian orbital paths, a NASA Tycho starfield skybox, and the Deep Space Network live telemetry panel. Additional layers in active development include dwarf planets, major moons, active spacecraft trajectories (Voyager, Juno, Parker, New Horizons, JUICE, Europa Clipper), historical missions, asteroid belt, near-Earth objects, comets, Trojan asteroids, Kuiper Belt, Oort Cloud, heliosphere, and Earth-Sun Lagrange points L1 through L5. Free accounts can activate up to 3 layers; Researcher tier and above gets unlimited access.
What data sources do you use?
All data comes from verified scientific and government sources:
  • NASA · Deep Space Network, mission ephemeris, NASA Tycho star catalog
  • JPL · SpaceObjectPresets, Small Body Database, Horizons ephemeris
  • ESA · Deep space mission data
  • spacekit.js · Open-source Three.js-based solar system rendering engine

What subscription tiers are available?
Free: 3 active layers and full orbital view. Researcher ($149/mo): full layer access including asteroid belt, comets, NEOs, Kuiper Belt, Oort Cloud, and heliosphere. Team ($499/mo): collaborative features and API access. Enterprise ($2,499/mo): tracking lists, custom data ingestion, and dedicated support. Subscriptions unlock features across all four Resource Systems platforms, not just this one. Cancel anytime with no long-term contracts.
Is the platform available on mobile?
Yes. SolarSystemResource is optimized for desktop and mobile browsers. The 3D solar system scene performs best on desktop with dedicated graphics hardware, but all core features including layer toggles, DSN feed, and authentication are fully functional on mobile. The sidebar collapses behind a toggle button on screens under 768px wide.
